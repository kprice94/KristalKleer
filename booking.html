Radio personality, entrepreneur, and executive producer of the media company “Underground”, Kristal Kleer has a grind that’s unmatched and a resume to match. The Chicago native realized her desire for radio in high school. She later moved to Atlanta, to attend Albany State University. During her time there, she landed a position as a guest on air personality on 96.3 WJIZ, Albany #1 Station for Hip-Hop and R&B. From there, she became the program director for the college’s radio station.

Kristal was the host of over 30 events during her time as a student, including the campus’s favorite event “Wassup Wednesday”. After obtaining her Mass Media of the Arts Degree, her company “Underground” has been committed to bridge the gap between hip-hop’s indie scene and the mainstream. Following in the footsteps of female urban journalists before her. Kristal hopes to carry the mantel left by moguls like Dorothy Brunson and Karen Civil, and take it to the next level. She has constantly working on breaking down barriers for women in the industry and is cementing her position as radio’s new leading lady.
